Working documents for fitting out the office as a multi camera data collection space. Five rooms surveyed and drawn, camera optics worked out for the Arducam OV9782 USB global shutter and the Raspberry Pi HQ with 6 mm and 16 mm lenses, a first pass placement plan, and a live 3D walkthrough where camera positions can be iterated and exported.

Camera working ranges: USB 112° for 0.8 to 2.8 m, HQ 6 mm 55° for 2.5 to 6.5 m, HQ 16 mm 22° for 6.5 to 14 m. In the walkthrough, select a camera to see its live view, move and aim it by clicking the floor, swap lens types, and export the layout as JSON when a plan is settled.

Measured room envelopes are exact; furniture and fittings are indicative and can be hidden in the walkthrough. Survey notes under each drawing sheet list every assumed dimension.

Camera study notes

  1. Optics: Arducam USB (OV9782, 1280 × 800 colour global shutter, 120 fps MJPG, UVC, external trigger capable) with the 130° diagonal M12 lens: approx. 112° × 78° effective (wide angle distortion reduces the rectilinear figure), resolving ~4.3 px/cm at 1 m. Pi HQ (IMX477, 4056 × 3040): with 6 mm lens approx. 55° × 43°, 39 px/cm at 1 m; with 16 mm lens approx. 22° × 17°, 103 px/cm at 1 m.
  2. Working ranges used for the wedges: USB 0.8–2.8 m (below ~1.5 px/cm beyond that), HQ 6 mm 2.5–6.5 m (≥ 6 px/cm), HQ 16 mm 6.5–14 m. The three ranges tile, so cells hand over cleanly.
  3. Larger rooms are split into capture cells (dashed): one HQ 6 mm per ~5 × 4.5 m cell, USB units over close fast work (desks, rig tables, bench train). The 16 mm is only proposed in the robotics room, shooting the full 13.4 m length from the east end to avoid backlight from the west window.
  4. Mounting: 230–270 above floor, tilted down 15–25°. Wedges are drawn in plan at mount height; floor coverage starts roughly 1 m out from the wall at these tilts. Aim for 15–20% overlap between neighbouring cells.
  5. The USB units are colour global shutter at 120 fps: use them where motion is fastest and closest. The HQ units are rolling shutter: prefer them for wider, calmer context views.
  6. Camera count this pass: 5 × USB (C1, C2, C5, C8, C12), 10 × HQ 6 mm (C3, C4, C6, C7, C9–C11, C13–C15), 1 × HQ 16 mm (C16). Positions are for taping out and testing, not final.
